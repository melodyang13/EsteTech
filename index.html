Interior Design

Interior designs defines how a space will appeal to one’s senses, how it will function,
and how it will look. It is said to be the art and science of a building to ensure a
healthy and aesthetically pleasing environment towards the the people in it. This is a
very important part of building a structure since it is not how the building is built
that makes people love it, but how it is designed.

Why Interior Design?

Getting into interior design can be extremely rewarding as it enables aspiring designers to help people transform other people’s homes into spaces that reflect their personalities and needs. The career allows designers to let their creativity run rampant while at the same time test their problem-solving and communication skills, providing a diverse and intellectually-stimulating career. The pay is also good with potential earnings ranging from $50,000 to more than $120,000 a year. For more information on this, click here to be guided by Bouchrika’s guide to interior design careers.

HISTORY

The Indigenous Tradition

At this time period, Interior Designs in the
Philippines were constructed of wood and bamboo
with minimal furntiture.

The Hispanic Tradition

During the colonization, Interior Designs in the
Philippines reflected the social, economic, and cultural
changes brought by the Spanish.

The American Colonial Tradition

During the American colonial period, apartments
or row houses were introduced. Art deco style mass-produced
furniture sets for the bedroom, dining room, and living
room became popular.

The Period of the Republics

At this time period, many interior designers
rose up and had influenced people with many differents styles
and designs with different personalities and elements.

The Modern Period

We are currently in the modern period where
we mix local and traditional touches of Filipino designs with
those of other countries. Many styles now exist and are available
to the people.
